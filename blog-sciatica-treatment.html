Sciatica: Why the Pain Goes Down Your Leg and What Physiotherapy Does About It

Aaditi Physio 6 min Sciatica & Spine

What Is Sciatica?

Sciatica refers to pain that travels along the path of the sciatic nerve — from the lower back through the buttock and down one leg, sometimes reaching the foot. It is caused by compression or irritation of the sciatic nerve or its roots.

The pain can range from a mild ache to a sharp, burning sensation or excruciating pain. Some patients also experience numbness, tingling, or weakness in the affected leg.

The Most Common Causes

Disc Herniation The most common cause. When the soft inner material of an intervertebral disc pushes through its outer ring, it can press on the nerve roots that form the sciatic nerve.

Spinal Stenosis Narrowing of the spinal canal that compresses the nerves. More common in people over 50.

Piriformis Syndrome The piriformis muscle in the buttock runs close to the sciatic nerve. When it spasms or tightens, it can compress the nerve — causing sciatica-like symptoms without any disc involvement.

Spondylolisthesis A condition where one vertebra slips forward over the one below it, pinching the nerve.

Why Getting the Right Diagnosis Matters

The treatment for disc-related sciatica is different from piriformis syndrome. A physiotherapist can differentiate between causes through clinical assessment — and this directly determines what treatment will and will not work for you.

Physiotherapy for Sciatica

At Aaditi Physiotherapy, sciatica treatment is always individualized based on the cause. Common approaches include:

  • Neural mobilization (nerve gliding exercises) to reduce nerve irritability - Manual therapy to address joint and disc issues - Core strengthening to reduce spinal load - Piriformis stretching and hip mobility work - Postural correction and ergonomic advice

    Most patients with acute sciatica see significant improvement within 4-8 sessions. Chronic cases may take longer but still respond well to physiotherapy.

    When to Seek Immediate Medical Attention

    Go to emergency services immediately if sciatica is accompanied by loss of bladder or bowel control, numbness in the groin or inner thighs, or progressive weakness in both legs. These can indicate cauda equina syndrome, a serious condition requiring urgent surgery.
