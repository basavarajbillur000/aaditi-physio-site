The IT Professional's Guide to Neck Pain: Causes, Fixes, and Prevention

Aaditi Physio 5 min Neck & Posture

Bangalore's Silent Epidemic

Walk into any tech company in Whitefield, Electronic City, or Koramangala and you'll find the same scene: rows of people hunched over laptops, heads pushed forward, shoulders rounded. By 6 PM, most of them have neck pain. By their mid-thirties, many have cervical spondylitis.

This is not inevitable. It is entirely preventable — and if it has already started, it is highly treatable with physiotherapy.

Why IT Professionals Get Neck Pain

Forward Head Posture For every inch your head moves forward from neutral alignment, it effectively adds 10 pounds of load on your cervical spine. Most desk workers carry their heads 2-3 inches forward — adding 20-30 pounds of extra load on structures not designed for it.

Static Muscle Holding Your neck muscles were designed for dynamic movement — not for holding one position for 8 hours. Sustained contraction without movement reduces blood flow, causes metabolic waste buildup, and leads to painful muscle knots (trigger points).

Screen Height A laptop screen placed flat on a desk forces you to look down constantly. The ideal screen height has your eyes level with the top third of the screen. Most people never set this up correctly.

5 Signs Your Neck Pain Needs Physiotherapy

1. Pain that has lasted more than 2 weeks 2. Headaches starting from the base of your skull 3. Numbness or tingling in your arms or fingers 4. Stiffness that is worse in the morning 5. Pain that limits your ability to turn your head fully

What We Do at Aaditi Physiotherapy

Our neck pain treatment protocol includes manual therapy to release joint restrictions, dry needling for deep muscle trigger points, postural correction exercises, and specific strengthening for the deep neck flexors — the muscles most responsible for holding your head in proper alignment.

Most desk workers see significant relief within 3-5 sessions. But more importantly, we teach you the habits and exercises to prevent recurrence.
